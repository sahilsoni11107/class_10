How To Tell Wild Animals


If ever you should go by chance
To jungles in the east;
And if there should to you advance
A large and tawny beast,
If he roars at you as you're dyin'
You'll know it is asian lion...

Or if some time when roaming around,
A noble wild beast greeta you,
With black stipes on a yellow ground,
Just notice if eats you.
This simple rule may help you to learn
The bengal tiger to discern.

If strolling forth, a beast you veiw,
Whose hide with spots is peppered,
As soon as he has lept on you,
You'll know it is leopard.
'Twill do no good to roar with pain,
he'll only lep and lep again.

if when you're walking round your yard
You meet a creature there,
Who hugs you very, very hard,
Be sure it is bear.
If you have any doubts, I guess
he'll give you just one more caress.

Though to distinguish beasts of prey
A novice might nonplus,
The crocodile you always may
Tell from the hyena thus:
Hyena come with merry smiles;
But if they weep they're Crocodiles.

The true chameleon is small,
A lizard sort of thing:
He hasn't any ear at all,
And not a single wing.
If thereis nothing on a tree,
Tis the chameleon you see.
-Carolyn Wells






Dust of Snow


The way a crow
shook down on me
The dust of snow
From a hemlock tree

Has given my heart
A change of mood
And saved some part
of a day I had rued.




Fire and Ice


Some say the world will end in fire
some say in Ice.
From what I've tasted of disire
I hold with those who favourfire.

But if it had to perish twice,
I think I know enough of hate
To say that for destruction ice
Is also great
And would suffice.





A Tiger in the Zoo


He stalks in his vivid stipes
The few steps of his cage,
On pads of velvet quiet,
in his cage.

He should be lurking in shadow,
sliding through long grass
Near the water hole
Where plump deer pass.

He sould be snarling around houses
At the jungle's edge,
Baring his white fangs, his claws,
Terrorising the vilage!

But he's locked in a concrete cell,
His strength behind bars,
Stalkin the length of his cage,
Ignoing visitors.

He hears the last voice at night,
The patroling cars,
And stares with his brilliant eyes
At the brilliant stars.
